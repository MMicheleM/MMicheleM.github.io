Week 3 Cultural Blog Post

Hearing Shereef talk about the iterations that DBC has seen has actually made me more excited about my time there. It’s cool to know that we’re doing in prep what once was Phase 1 and I can imagine that what we do in Phase 1 will soon become something future cohorts do before they hit Phase 0. Part of why I’m headed to DBC is because I really want to learn to code and while it’s true you can teach yourself I find it is, for my learning style, much easier to do it with some guidelines and teachers. That said I believe it is fully on me to make it my own experience. Much like with jobs wherever you go there you are - it’s you who are the constant. Figuring out how to do ‘learning verticals’ with code is what DBC is all about and is what tech is often about - so the onus is on us to figure out how to scale those walls in front of us.

For me learning verticals has been my approach to my professional life and I find it engaging and rewarding (most of the time.) However as a hiring manager I know that there are a lot of people out there who don’t do verticals, they only do learning curves. There is nothing wrong with that you simply have to change your approach and expectations as a manager. The idea that I’ll be immersed in a program with nothing but people who are excited by verticals is an interesting proposition. I cannot imagine what that would be like as I have encountered far fewer of the vertically inclined than I have of the curve group. Shereef's talk prompted this ‘aha’ moment and I guess my expectation changed slightly to incorporate the idea of being surrounded by ‘like learners’.

As for nervousness - all new environments make me nervous. A large part of that is my introverted and shy nature - which I've learned to cover pretty well over the years. That said this entire venture makes me nervous - in both good and stressful ways. What came up for me was the continuing realization that DBC seems like it may be a truly solid cultural fit for me - something I wasn't expecting. I’m excited to see if that proves out.

Circa May 4, 2014
